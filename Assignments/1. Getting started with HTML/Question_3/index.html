Chandrayaan-3

Chandrayaan-3 is the third lunar mission of the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). It was launched on 14 July 2023 from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre in Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh. The mission consists of a lunar orbiter, a lander, and a rover.

The orbiter will orbit the Moon for one year, mapping its surface and studying its composition. The lander, named Vikram, will touch down in the Moon's southern polar region. The rover, named Pragyan, will then roll out of the lander and explore the surrounding area.

The main objectives of Chandrayaan-3 are to:

  • Soft land on the Moon's south polar region
  • Deploy the Pragyan rover to explore the lunar surface
  • Study the composition of the lunar soil and rocks
  • Search for water ice in the lunar polar regions
  • Conduct experiments to understand the lunar environment

Chandrayaan-3 is a major milestone in India's space program. It is the first Indian mission to attempt a soft landing on the Moon's south polar region. The success of the mission will demonstrate India's technological prowess and its ability to undertake complex space missions.

The Chandrayaan-3 mission was a success. The Vikram lander successfully touched down in the Moon's southern polar region on August 23, 2023. The Pragyan rover was then deployed from the lander and began exploring the surrounding area. The mission is expected to last for one year.

The success of Chandrayaan-3 is a major achievement for India. It is the first Indian mission to successfully land on the Moon's south polar region. The mission will help scientists to better understand the Moon's composition and environment. It will also help India to develop its space technology and capabilities.

Aditya-L1

Aditya-L1 is a solar observation mission of the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). It was launched on 28 December 2022 from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre in Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh. The mission is named after Aditya, the Hindu god of the Sun.

Aditya-L1 will be placed in a halo orbit around the L1 Lagrange point, which is located about 1.5 million kilometers from Earth. The L1 point is a gravitationally stable location where the gravitational forces of the Sun and Earth are balanced. This will allow Aditya-L1 to stay in a fixed position relative to the Sun, so that it can continuously study the solar atmosphere.

The main objectives of Aditya-L1 are to:

  • Study the solar corona, the outermost layer of the Sun's atmosphere
  • Understand the mechanisms of solar flares and coronal mass ejections
  • Investigate the relationship between solar activity and space weather
  • Develop new technologies for solar observation

  • A coronagraph, which blocks out the light from the solar disk so that the corona can be studied
  • A spectrograph, which can be used to study the composition of the solar atmosphere
  • A magnetometer, which can be used to measure the magnetic field of the Sun
  • A particle detector, which can be used to study the particles emitted by the Sun

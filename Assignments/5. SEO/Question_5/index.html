Q. Define Favicon and give an illustrative example.

A.

A favicon, short for "favorite icon," is a small, typically square or rectangular graphic icon that is associated with a website or web page. It is displayed in the browser's address bar, next to the page's title in browser tabs, and in bookmarks or favorites lists. Favicon images are usually very small, often 16x16 pixels or 32x32 pixels in size, and are intended to visually represent the website or provide a recognizable logo or branding element for the site.

Favicons serve several purposes:

  • Branding: Favicons help reinforce a website's branding by displaying a recognizable logo or symbol associated with the site.
  • Visual Identification: They make it easier for users to quickly identify and switch between open tabs in their web browsers.
  • Bookmarking: When users bookmark a page, the favicon is often displayed alongside the page title in the bookmarks list, making it easier to locate the desired bookmark.
  • Professionalism: Including a favicon adds a polished and professional touch to a website, enhancing its overall user experience.
